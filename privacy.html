Overview

Plain English summary: Owelet collects only what it needs to run the app. We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with advertisers. Your debt records are private to you. You can delete your account and all your data at any time.

This Privacy Policy explains how Owelet ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information when you use the Owelet mobile application and any related services (collectively, the "Service").

By downloading or using Owelet, you agree to the collection and use of information as described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the Service.

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information in accordance with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019, issued by the National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA), and other applicable data protection laws.

Information We Collect

1. Information You Provide Directly

When you create an account and use Owelet, you may provide us with:

  • Account information: Your full name and email address, provided when you create an Owelet account.
  • Business information: Your business name and business phone number, if you choose to add these for receipt branding (Pro feature). This is optional.
  • Debt records: The names, phone numbers, amounts, descriptions, due dates, and payment histories of your credit customers that you enter into the app.
  • Payment records: Details of payments made by your customers, including amounts, dates, and any notes you attach.
  • Notes and descriptions: Any additional text you enter when creating or updating debt records.

2. Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, we may automatically collect certain technical information:

  • Device information: Your device type, operating system version, and unique device identifiers. This is used to ensure the app functions correctly on your device.
  • Usage data: Information about how you use the app, such as which features you use and when you use them. This helps us improve the product.
  • Error logs: Crash reports and error logs that help us identify and fix technical problems. These may include device state at the time of a crash but do not include your personal debt records.
  • App version: The version of Owelet you are running, so we can provide relevant support.

3. Information from Third Parties

If you use Google Sign-In or another third-party authentication method to create your account (where available), we receive basic profile information from that provider, typically your name and email address, as permitted by their terms. We do not receive your passwords or payment information from these providers.

4. Contact Information (with Your Permission)

If you use the "Import from Contacts" feature, Owelet requests permission to access your device's contact list. See the Contact Access section below for full details on how this works.

Data Storage and Security

Where Your Data is Stored

Your Owelet data is stored in two places:

  • On your device: Debt records are stored locally on your phone using an encrypted local database (Hive). This allows Owelet to work fully offline, even without an internet connection.
  • In the cloud: When you are connected to the internet, your data is automatically synced to our secure cloud database hosted by Supabase in the European Union (Frankfurt, Germany). This enables data backup and access across multiple devices.

How We Protect Your Data

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information:

  • Encryption in transit: All data transmitted between the app and our servers is encrypted using TLS (Transport Layer Security).
  • Encryption at rest: Your data stored in our cloud database is encrypted at rest by Supabase.
  • Row-Level Security: Our database is configured so that each user can only access their own data. It is technically impossible for one Owelet user to view another user's debt records.
  • Authentication: Access to your account requires email and password verification. You can optionally add a PIN lock for additional device-level security.
  • PKCE authentication flow: We use the Proof Key for Code Exchange (PKCE) standard for secure mobile authentication.

No Security is Absolute

While we take data security seriously and implement strong protections, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of your data. If you believe your account has been compromised, please contact us immediately at info@owelet.ng.

Contact Access

Owelet includes an optional "Import from Contacts" feature that allows you to quickly fill in a debtor's name and phone number by selecting them from your phone's contact list.

What We Access

When you use this feature, Owelet requests permission to read your device contacts. We access only the name and phone number of the specific contact you select.

What We Do Not Do

  • We do not upload your contact list to our servers
  • We do not store your contacts beyond the single name and phone number you choose to add to a debt record
  • We do not scan, analyse, or retain your full contact list
  • We do not use your contacts for marketing or share them with any third party

This Feature is Optional

You can use Owelet fully without granting contact access. You can type debtor names and phone numbers manually at any time. If you previously granted contacts permission and wish to revoke it, you can do so in your phone's app settings at any time.

Payment Data

Owelet Pro subscriptions are processed by Paystack, a third-party payment processor.

What Owelet Receives

When you subscribe, Paystack sends us a webhook notification confirming your payment was successful. This notification includes:

  • Your email address (to identify your account)
  • The subscription plan you chose (monthly or annual)
  • A transaction reference number
  • The amount charged

We use this information only to activate and manage your Pro subscription in Owelet.

What Owelet Does Not Receive or Store

  • Your card number, CVV, or expiry date
  • Your bank account details
  • Any other sensitive payment credentials

All sensitive payment data is handled exclusively by Paystack on their secure PCI-DSS compliant infrastructure. Please review Paystack's Privacy Policy for details on how they handle your payment information.

Subscription Records

We maintain records of your subscription history — when you subscribed, which plan, and when it expires, for the purpose of managing your access to Pro features and for financial record-keeping as required by law.

Your Rights

Under the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) and applicable law, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

Right to Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you. You can access most of your data directly through the Owelet app. For a complete data export, contact us at info@owelet.ng.

Right to Rectification

You have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data. You can update your account information directly in the app under Settings. For other corrections, contact us.

Right to Erasure

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data and Owelet account. To delete your account and all associated data, contact us at info@owelet.ng with the subject line "Account Deletion Request." We will complete the deletion within 7 business days and confirm when done.

Please note that we may retain certain information as required by law (such as subscription payment records for tax purposes) for the legally required period even after account deletion.

Right to Data Portability

You have the right to receive your data in a portable format. Owelet supports this through our built-in JSON and CSV export features, accessible in Settings → Export, Backup & Reports. You can export all your debt records at any time.

Right to Object

You have the right to object to certain types of processing, such as processing for direct marketing purposes. Owelet does not conduct direct marketing using your personal data.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where we process your data based on your consent (such as contact access), you may withdraw that consent at any time through your device settings without affecting the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.

How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at info@owelet.ng. We will respond to all requests within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before processing your request.

Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active or as necessary to provide you with the Service.

  • Account data: Retained for the duration of your account and deleted within 7 business days of an account deletion request.
  • Debt and payment records: Retained as long as your account exists. Deleted when you delete your account.
  • Subscription records: Retained for 7 years after the subscription ends, as required by Nigerian financial record-keeping regulations.
  • Error logs: Automatically deleted after 90 days.
  • Usage analytics: Aggregated and anonymised after 12 months. Individual usage data is deleted after 12 months.

When data reaches the end of its retention period, we securely delete or anonymise it.
